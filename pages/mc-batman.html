BATMAN

At this point, pretty much everybody knows Batman's origin story. Born to wealthy parents, Thomas and Martha, his entire life would be turned upside down when at the tender age of ten he would witness them both gunned down by a mugger in a Gotham back alley after leaving a movie theater. As he would grow older, Bruce would find it impossible to come to grips with the idea that any innocent person could fall victim to such a malicious crime. This would send him on a soul searching journey around the world where he would be trained in the most extreme arts of combat and learn to push his mind and body beyond their limit and make himself unbreakable. Armed with this new knowledge and resiliance, he would return to Gotham City and fight crime as its protector, the Batman.

As a kid, Batman was the first superhero that really grabbed my attention. It started with the animated series. I loved the noir asthetic and the way they portrayed Gotham City so realistically. I always hated the idea of superheroes being so passive in the way that they attacked villians. For this reason I loved Batman because he was always willing to meet a brutal villian with that same type of brutality because, at the end of the day, they were evil villains intent on killing innocent people. I love the way that Batman is almost never at a disadvantage and seemingly able to adapt to any circumstance in order to prevail against his foes. Sometimes his adaptability can venture on the side of the rediculously unbelievable, but, he is the BATMAN!
